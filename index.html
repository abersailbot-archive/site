Welcome To AberSailbot's Sailbot2013 Site!

We are a group of Undergraduate Computer Science and Physics students at Aberystwyth University. We are building an autonomous sailing robot for the Sailbot competition.

We've been working on our entry to Sailbot2013 since October last year, and it's almost ready to go, but now we need the money to actually get the boat, and our team out to the USA to compete, and we'd love for you to help us get there!

Post made at 15:25, 11/03/13 by Louis Taylor
Yet Another Really Busy Day

Today, once again, we had a really productive day. We drilled all of the waterproof connector holes, and fitted all but 2 of the connectors because the delivery hasn't come yet. We added an LED to the power distribution board so that we can see when it's on better. We also considered putting a waterproof switch outside of the boxes to make it easier to turn the power on and off. Louis tested and rewrote the GPS code to make it more efficient and less buggy. We cabled the XBee box up to the main box.

Importantly we tested the sail winch and rudder servo with the RC remote, which showed we were getting an abnormal amount of interference in the connection. We diagnosed the problem as either radio interference from the room, power interference from the regulators/batteries, which could be fixed by some bigger resistors, or interference down the cable attaching the servo's to the other boxes, which can be fixed by shortening the cable.
